About

Ludovico Einaudi, born on 23 November 1955, is an Italian pianist and composer.
Trained at the Conservatorio Verdi in Milan, he began his career as a classical composer, later incorporating other styles and genres such as pop, rock, folk, and world music. He has released a number of solo albums for piano and other instruments, notably I Giorni in 2001, Nightbook in 2009, and In a Time Lapse in 2013.

Einaudi has composed the scores for a number of films and television productions, including This Is England,
The Intouchables, I'm Still Here, the TV miniseries Doctor Zhivago, and Acquario (1996), for which he won the Grolla d'oro award. On 1 March 2019, the composer announced a seven-part project named Seven Days Walking, which was released over the course of seven months in 2019.
